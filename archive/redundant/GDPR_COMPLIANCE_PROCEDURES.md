## 1. Introduction to GDPR {#introduction}

### What is GDPR?
The General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) is a comprehensive data privacy law that:
- Protects EU residents' personal data
- Applies to organizations processing EU residents' data
- Requires explicit consent and transparent processing
- Grants individuals extensive rights over their data

### GDPR Scope for Pitchey
- **Global Application**: Affects all Pitchey operations
- **User Base**: EU residents using our platform
- **Data Types**: Profile data, pitch content, communication records
- **Legal Basis**: Consent, contract performance, legitimate interests

- **Administrative Fines**: Up to â‚¬20 million or 4% of annual turnover
- **Reputational Damage**: Loss of user trust and market position
- **Operational Impact**: Business disruption and legal costs

## 2. Data Processing Principles {#principles}

### Six Core Principles

#### 1. Lawfulness, Fairness, and Transparency
**Implementation**:
- Clear privacy notices at data collection
- Explicit consent for non-essential processing
- Regular privacy policy updates
- User-friendly privacy communications

**Staff Actions**:
- Verify legal basis before data collection
- Provide clear information about data use
- Document all processing activities
- Update users on processing changes

#### 2. Purpose Limitation
**Implementation**:
- Specific, explicit, and legitimate purposes
- No further processing incompatible with original purpose
- Regular purpose review and documentation
- Clear purpose communication to users

**Staff Actions**:
- Define clear purposes before collection
- Verify purpose alignment for new uses
- Document purpose changes
- Obtain additional consent when needed

#### 3. Data Minimization
**Implementation**:
- Collect only necessary data
- Regular data audits and cleanup
- Purpose-driven data collection
- Automated data reduction

**Staff Actions**:
- Question necessity of each data point
- Remove unnecessary data collection
- Regular data inventory reviews
- Challenge data requests

#### 4. Accuracy
**Implementation**:
- Data validation at entry
- User correction mechanisms
- Regular accuracy reviews
- Error correction procedures

**Staff Actions**:
- Verify data accuracy at collection
- Provide user correction tools
- Promptly correct inaccuracies
- Maintain correction audit trails

#### 5. Storage Limitation
**Implementation**:
- Defined retention periods
- Automated deletion processes
- Regular data purging
- Archive policies

**Staff Actions**:
- Apply retention schedules
- Monitor data age
- Execute deletion procedures
- Document retention decisions

#### 6. Integrity and Confidentiality
**Implementation**:
- Encryption at rest and in transit
- Access controls and monitoring
- Regular security assessments
- Staff security training

**Staff Actions**:
- Use secure systems only
- Follow access control procedures
- Report security incidents
- Maintain confidentiality

## 3. Data Subject Rights Procedures {#rights}

### Right to Be Informed

#### When Required
- At data collection point
- Before processing begins
- When processing purposes change
- Upon direct data request

#### Implementation Steps
1. **Immediate Response**: Acknowledge receipt within 24 hours
2. **Information Compilation**: Gather all relevant processing information
3. **Clear Communication**: Use plain language, avoid legal jargon
4. **Complete Disclosure**: Include all required GDPR information
5. **Delivery**: Provide information within 1 month

#### Required Information
- Identity of data controller
- Purpose of processing
- Legal basis for processing
- Categories of personal data
- Recipients of data
- Retention periods
- Data subject rights
- Contact information

### Right of Access

#### Process Flow
1. **Identity Verification**: Confirm requestor identity
2. **Scope Clarification**: Determine data to include
3. **Data Compilation**: Gather from all systems
4. **Review and Redaction**: Remove third-party data
5. **Secure Delivery**: Provide via secure channel

#### Response Timeline
- **Acknowledgment**: Within 24 hours
- **Complete Response**: Within 1 month
- **Extension**: Additional 2 months if complex (with justification)

#### Deliverable Format
- Structured, commonly used format
- Machine-readable when requested
- Includes metadata and context
- Secure access method

### Right to Rectification

#### Validation Process
1. **Request Assessment**: Evaluate accuracy claims
2. **Evidence Review**: Analyze supporting documentation
3. **Internal Verification**: Check against authoritative sources
4. **Impact Analysis**: Assess correction consequences
5. **Implementation**: Apply corrections across all systems

#### Communication Requirements
- Notify all recipients of corrected data
- Update shared data with third parties
- Document correction reasoning
- Confirm completion to requestor

### Right to Erasure (Right to be Forgotten)

#### Eligibility Assessment
**Valid Reasons for Erasure**:
- Data no longer necessary for original purpose
- Consent withdrawal (consent-based processing)
- Objection to processing (legitimate interest basis)
- Unlawful processing
- Legal compliance obligation

**Grounds for Refusal**:
- Freedom of expression exercise
- Legal compliance requirements
- Public interest tasks
- Legitimate interests override

#### Erasure Process
1. **Request Evaluation**: Assess eligibility criteria
2. **Impact Analysis**: Evaluate business and legal implications
3. **Stakeholder Notification**: Inform relevant parties
4. **System Erasure**: Remove from all systems
5. **Third-party Coordination**: Request erasure from partners
6. **Verification**: Confirm complete removal
7. **Documentation**: Record erasure completion

### Right to Restrict Processing

#### Restriction Triggers
- Accuracy dispute during verification
- Unlawful processing (user prefers restriction over erasure)
- Data no longer needed but required for legal claims
- Objection pending legitimate interest assessment

#### Implementation
- Mark data as restricted in all systems
- Limit processing to storage only
- Obtain consent for any processing
- Notify before lifting restrictions

### Right to Data Portability

#### Scope and Conditions
- Applies to automated processing
- Based on consent or contract
- User-provided or user-generated data
- Machine-readable format

#### Delivery Options
- Secure download link
- Direct transmission to another controller
- Structured data format (JSON, CSV, XML)
- API-based transfer

### Right to Object

#### Objection Types
**Direct Marketing**: Absolute right, no exceptions
**Legitimate Interest Processing**: Requires compelling grounds assessment
**Public Task Processing**: Limited circumstances
**Research Processing**: Particular situation required

#### Response Process
1. **Immediate Cessation**: Stop objected processing
2. **Assessment**: Evaluate compelling legitimate grounds
3. **Decision**: Continue, modify, or cease processing
4. **Notification**: Inform user of decision and reasoning
5. **Alternative Measures**: Implement if processing continues

## 4. Consent Management {#consent}

### Valid Consent Criteria

#### GDPR Requirements
- **Freely Given**: Real choice, no detriment for refusal
- **Specific**: Clear purpose identification
- **Informed**: Understand what consenting to
- **Unambiguous**: Clear affirmative action

#### Implementation Standards
- **Granular Choices**: Category-specific consent options
- **Easy Withdrawal**: Simple withdrawal mechanisms
- **Record Keeping**: Detailed consent logs
- **Regular Review**: Periodic consent validation

### Consent Collection Process

#### Pre-Collection Planning
1. **Purpose Definition**: Clear processing purposes
2. **Legal Basis Assessment**: Verify consent appropriateness
3. **Interface Design**: User-friendly consent mechanisms
4. **Technical Implementation**: Consent management systems

#### Collection Implementation
1. **Clear Information**: Explain processing purposes
2. **Granular Options**: Category-specific choices
3. **Active Consent**: Require positive action
4. **Record Creation**: Log consent details
5. **Confirmation**: Provide consent summary

#### Consent Records
Must include:
- Who consented
- When consent given
- What information provided
- How consent given
- Evidence of consent action

### Consent Withdrawal

#### Withdrawal Mechanisms
- **Account Settings**: Online preference management
- **Direct Request**: Email or support channel
- **Unsubscribe Links**: For marketing communications
- **API Endpoints**: Programmatic withdrawal

#### Processing Withdrawal
1. **Immediate Effect**: Stop processing upon withdrawal
2. **System Updates**: Update all relevant systems
3. **Confirmation**: Acknowledge withdrawal to user
4. **Alternative Legal Basis**: Assess other processing grounds
5. **Record Keeping**: Log withdrawal details

## 5. Incident Response {#incidents}

### Data Breach Classification

#### Personal Data Breach Definition
Unauthorized or unlawful processing, accidental loss, destruction, or damage to personal data.

#### Breach Types
- **Confidentiality Breach**: Unauthorized disclosure
- **Integrity Breach**: Unauthorized alteration
- **Availability Breach**: Accidental loss or destruction

#### Risk Assessment Criteria
- **Number of Affected Individuals**: Scale of impact
- **Data Sensitivity**: Type of personal data
- **Likelihood of Harm**: Probability of adverse consequences
- **Severity of Consequences**: Magnitude of potential damage

### Breach Response Process

#### Phase 1: Detection and Initial Response (0-1 hours)
1. **Breach Discovery**: Identify security incident
2. **Initial Assessment**: Evaluate scope and severity
3. **Containment**: Implement immediate safeguards
4. **Team Activation**: Notify incident response team
5. **Documentation Start**: Begin incident log

#### Phase 2: Investigation and Assessment (1-24 hours)
1. **Detailed Investigation**: Analyze breach circumstances
2. **Impact Assessment**: Evaluate affected individuals and data
3. **Risk Analysis**: Assess likelihood and severity of harm
4. **Evidence Preservation**: Secure relevant evidence
5. **Preliminary Report**: Document initial findings

#### Phase 3: Notification (24-72 hours)
1. **Regulatory Notification**: Report to supervisory authority (if required)
2. **Individual Notification**: Contact affected data subjects (if high risk)
3. **Internal Communication**: Update senior management
4. **Stakeholder Notification**: Inform relevant partners
5. **Public Communication**: Issue statements if necessary

#### Phase 4: Remediation and Recovery (Ongoing)
1. **Security Measures**: Implement additional safeguards
2. **Affected Individual Support**: Provide assistance and guidance
3. **Monitoring**: Watch for ongoing risks or exploitation
4. **System Recovery**: Restore normal operations
5. **Follow-up Communication**: Update stakeholders as needed

#### Phase 5: Review and Improvement (Post-incident)
1. **Incident Analysis**: Comprehensive review of response
2. **Root Cause Analysis**: Identify underlying causes
3. **Process Improvement**: Update procedures and controls
4. **Training Updates**: Revise staff training programs
5. **Documentation**: Complete incident record

### Notification Requirements

#### Supervisory Authority Notification
**Timeline**: Within 72 hours of discovery
**Required Information**:
- Nature of breach
- Categories and number of affected individuals
- Categories and number of personal data records
- Likely consequences
- Measures taken or proposed

#### Individual Notification
**Trigger**: High risk to rights and freedoms
**Timeline**: Without undue delay
**Required Information**:
- Nature of breach in plain language
- Contact information for inquiries
- Likely consequences
- Measures taken or proposed
- Recommendations for protection
